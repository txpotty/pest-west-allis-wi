Signs You Might Need Raccoon Removal Services

Recognizing the signs of a raccoon infestation early can save you a lot of trouble and expense down the line. Raccoons are nocturnal, so you might not always see them during the day, but their presence often leaves clear evidence.

Noises in the Attic or Walls

One of the most common indicators of raccoons in your home is unusual noises coming from your attic, walls, or crawl spaces, particularly at night. These sounds can range from scratching and scurrying to thumping and growling. If you hear these noises, especially soon after dusk or before dawn, it’s a strong sign of active wildlife. Raccoons are relatively large animals, so their movements within confined spaces can be quite audible. Ignoring these sounds can lead to further damage and a more established infestation.

Overturned Trash Cans and Scattered Garbage

Raccoons are notorious for raiding trash cans. They are intelligent and strong enough to open lids and tip over containers in search of food. If you consistently find your trash cans overturned and garbage scattered around your property, especially in West Allis, it’s a clear sign that raccoons are frequenting your yard. This not only creates a mess but also attracts other pests and can pose a health risk.

Damage to Your Property

Raccoons can cause significant damage to your home and property as they attempt to gain entry or build nests. This can include torn shingles, damaged soffits, bent gutters, ripped screens, and even structural damage to decks, sheds, or porches. They may also tear up insulation in attics for nesting material. The cost of repairing this damage can be substantial if not addressed promptly. Our residential services encompass identifying and addressing this damage.

Presence of Feces and Urine

Raccoons often establish communal latrine sites, which are areas where they repeatedly defecate. These latrines can be found in attics, on decks, under sheds, or other elevated surfaces. Raccoon feces can contain harmful pathogens like roundworm eggs, which are highly contagious to humans and pets. The presence of large piles of dark, tubular droppings is a significant sign of a raccoon infestation and a serious health concern that requires professional cleanup and wildlife control.

Seeing Raccoons During the Day

While raccoons are primarily nocturnal, seeing one during the day can sometimes indicate that it is sick or nursing young nearby. While it's not always a sign of a problem, a raccoon that appears disoriented, aggressive, or unnaturally bold during daylight hours should be treated with caution. However, a mother raccoon with a den nearby may venture out during the day to forage for food for her young.

Strong Odors

A persistent, foul odor emanating from your attic, walls, or other areas can be a sign of raccoon presence, particularly if it's a musty smell combined with the odor of urine and feces. As the infestation grows and waste accumulates, the smell can become quite overpowering and difficult to ignore. These odors not only make your home unpleasant but can also attract other pests. Professional removal and sanitation are crucial to eliminate these odors and the source of the problem.

Damaged Gardens or Crops

If you have a garden, fruit trees, or vegetable patch, you might notice damage or missing produce. Raccoons are opportunistic feeders and will readily raid gardens for fruits, vegetables, and even pet food left outdoors. Signs of digging, partially eaten produce, or missing items from your garden are all potential indicators of raccoon activity.

Visual Sightings

The most obvious sign is, of course, seeing a raccoon on your property. While a single sighting might not necessarily mean an infestation, frequent sightings, especially near your home or outbuildings, suggest they are using your property as a regular food source or shelter. If you see raccoons entering or exiting a specific point on your home, it’s almost certain you have a den inside and require professional raccoon removal services in the Wauwatosa or Greenfield area.

Disturbed Pet Behaviors

Your pets might react to the presence of raccoons, even if you don't see them. Dogs might bark excessively at certain areas of the house or yard, especially at night. Cats might become agitated or try to avoid certain parts of the home. Pay attention to your pets' behaviors, as they can sometimes alert you to wildlife issues before you notice other signs.

Tracks and Paw Prints

Raccoons have distinctive paw prints that look somewhat like small human hands. You might find their tracks in soft soil, mud, dust, or even snow around your property. Look for four toes on the front paws and five toes on the back paws. Finding these tracks can help confirm the presence of raccoons and indicate their travel paths.

Damage to Fencing or Outbuildings

Raccoons are excellent climbers and are strong enough to damage wooden fences or screens on sheds in their attempts to gain access. Inspect your fencing and outbuildings for signs of climbing, claw marks, or damaged areas that could be used as entry points. This is particularly important for commercial properties as well.

Presence of Kits (Baby Raccoons)

If you hear distinct chittering or whining sounds coming from your attic or walls, especially in the spring or early summer, it could indicate the presence of baby raccoons, also known as kits. Mother raccoons give birth and raise their young in secure, sheltered locations. Finding a den of kits requires specialized removal techniques to ensure both the mother and young are safely relocated.

Unusual Activity Around Your Chimney

Chimneys are common den sites for raccoons. They are warm, sheltered, and provide a secure place to raise young. If you hear noises coming from your chimney, see raccoons around the top, or notice damage to the chimney cap, it's highly likely you have a raccoon issue in this area. Professional removal is essential as attempting to remove them yourself can be dangerous and lead to the animals entering your living space.

Neighboring Properties Experiencing Issues

Wildlife problems can often affect multiple properties in a neighborhood. If your neighbors in Milwaukee, Brookfield, or Oak Creek have reported raccoon sightings or damage, it increases the likelihood that your property may also be affected. Raccoons have established territories, and if your area provides attractive resources, they will likely visit multiple homes.

Are There Any Health Risks Associated With Raccoon Infestations?

Yes, raccoons can carry several diseases that are transmissible to humans and pets, including rabies, leptospirosis, and raccoon roundworm. Raccoon roundworm eggs can be present in their feces and remain viable in the environment for years. Inhaling or ingesting these microscopic eggs can cause serious neurological damage. Proper sanitation and cleanup of areas contaminated by raccoon waste are essential health precautions. This is why our sanitation services are a critical part of our raccoon removal service in West Allis, WI.

Raccoon activity can occur year-round, but certain times of the year are more prone to specific issues. Spring and summer are breeding seasons, and mother raccoons often seek safe, den sites to raise their young, which can lead to infestations in attics or chimneys. Fall and winter can also see raccoons seeking shelter from the cold in homes and buildings. We provide raccoon removal services throughout the year to address your needs whenever they arise.

How Can I Tell if a Raccoon is Sick?

A sick or rabid raccoon may exhibit unusual behaviors, such as being active during the day, appearing disoriented, staggering, or showing aggression without provocation.
